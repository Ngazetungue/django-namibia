About Django Namibia

The official community for Django developers in Namibia, fostering collaboration and knowledge sharing.

Our Story

How It All Began

Django Namibia was founded in 2017 by a group of passionate developers under the umbrella of the Python Software Association of Namibia (PyNAM). We recognized the need for a dedicated Django community in Namibia.

As an official working group of PyNAM, what started as informal meetups in Windhoek has grown into a national community with members across the country. Our first official event brought together 50 developers, and since then we've been organizing workshops, hackathons, and networking events to grow the Django ecosystem in Namibia.

Our PyNAM Affiliation

Django Namibia operates as a special interest group under the Python Software Association of Namibia (PyNAM), the official body representing Python developers in Namibia. This affiliation connects us with the broader Python community while allowing us to focus specifically on Django development.

Our Growth

With the support of PyNAM, we've grown to over 200 members across Namibia. Our community includes beginners learning web development, experienced Django developers, and companies building products with Django.

Through our PyNAM affiliation, we've partnered with local tech companies, educational institutions, and the global Django community to bring world-class resources and opportunities to Namibian developers.

Our Mission & Values

Community First

We believe in creating an inclusive, welcoming environment for all developers regardless of their experience level.

Education

We're committed to providing learning resources and opportunities to help developers grow their Django skills.

Collaboration

We foster collaboration between developers, companies, and educational institutions to build Namibia's tech ecosystem.

Our Mission Statement

"To build and nurture a thriving community of Django developers in Namibia that drives innovation, shares knowledge, and creates opportunities for professional growth while contributing to the global Django ecosystem."
