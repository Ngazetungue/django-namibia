Community Code of Conduct

Our shared commitment to creating a welcoming, respectful environment for all Django enthusiasts in Namibia

This isn't just rules - it's our community's foundation. By participating in Django Namibia, you're helping build a space where everyone can thrive, learn, and contribute without fear of harassment or discrimination.

Our Community Promise

In the spirit of Ubuntu and Namibia's diverse culture, we pledge to foster an environment where all participants - regardless of background or experience level - feel valued and empowered. Whether you're a student in Windhoek, a developer in Swakopmund, or joining us online from anywhere in Namibia, you belong here.

We Embrace

  • Kindness and constructive feedback
  • Diverse perspectives and experiences
  • Collaboration over competition

We Reject

  • Harassment in any form
  • Discriminatory language or behavior
  • Exclusionary practices

Your Safety Matters

If you experience or witness misconduct: You're not alone. Our response team is here to help with discretion and care. Reports can be made anonymously if preferred.

Namibian Context

We recognize Namibia's unique cultural diversity and post-colonial context. Our conduct standards specifically address:

  • Respect for all ethnic groups and languages
  • Sensitivity to historical inequalities
  • Encouraging participation from underrepresented groups
